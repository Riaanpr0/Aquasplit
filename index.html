A simple solution for outdoor survival and clean water access

In outdoor environments like hiking or camping, clean drinking water can be hard to find. Natural sources such as rivers or ponds may contain harmful impurities. Our project investigates whether simple distillation can purify dirty water to make it drinkable.

We hypothesized that if we distilled dirty water, the output would be clear, safe, and neutral in pH. This is because distillation removes solids and most contaminants.

We heated 100 mL of a sand and water mixture until it evaporated. The vapor condensed and was collected in a clean container. We controlled the volume, heating time, and collection setup for consistency.

The AquaSplit device effectively purifies dirty water using distillation. The resulting water is clear, neutral in pH, and safe for consumption. This method can be life-saving in survival situations, emergency zones, or remote areas.
