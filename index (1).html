Over 2 billion people globally lack access to safely managed drinking water. In emergency situations, natural disasters, or outdoor expeditions, access to clean water becomes critical. Contaminated water sources can carry harmful pathogens, sediments, and toxins.

AquaSplit is a student-designed solution that demonstrates how simple distillation can convert dirty or sandy water into safe, drinkable water‚Äîmaking it a potential life-saving tool in the wild or during crisis response.

üß† Hypothesis

If we heat dirty sand-water using a controlled distillation setup, then the vapor collected will condense into purified water with a neutral pH and no visible impurities, as distillation effectively separates contaminants from water molecules.

üî¨ Experimental Setup

We designed a basic distillation system using school lab materials. Here‚Äôs what we did:

  • Input: 100 mL of sand-water mixture
  • Heat Source: Bunsen burner (controlled heat)
  • Collection: Glass bowl with cooling area for condensation
  • Measurement Tools: pH test strips, stopwatch, and collection cylinder

We controlled variables such as input volume, heating time, and container size to ensure reliable results.

üîé Scientific Explanation

Distillation works by heating water until it turns into vapor, leaving contaminants behind. This vapor is then cooled and condensed into liquid water. Since most contaminants (like dirt and bacteria) do not evaporate, the resulting water is purified.

In our experiment, the neutral pH and clear appearance of the condensed water confirms that distillation removed most physical and chemical impurities effectively.
